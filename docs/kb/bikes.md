# Bikes on the MBTA

## Subway
Folding bikes are allowed on the subway at all times. Full-size bikes are allowed on the Red,
Orange, and Blue Lines except during weekday rush hours (roughly 7–10 AM and 4–7 PM). Bikes are
never allowed on the Green Line or on the Mattapan Trolley because of limited space.

## Bus
Most MBTA buses have front-mounted racks that hold two or three bikes, available first-come,
first-served at no extra charge. The bus operator cannot store bikes inside the vehicle.

## Commuter Rail and ferry
Bikes are allowed on most Commuter Rail trains outside of crowded peak trains, at the conductor's
discretion. Ferries allow a limited number of bikes per trip. Always board through the doors marked
for bikes and yield priority space to wheelchair users.

## Bluebikes
Bluebikes is the regional bike-share system serving Boston, Cambridge, Somerville, and nearby
communities. It is separate from the MBTA but is often used for the first or last mile of a transit
trip. Stations are docking-based, and passes are sold separately from MBTA fares.

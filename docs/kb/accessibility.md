# MBTA Accessibility

## Accessible stations and vehicles
All MBTA buses are low-floor and wheelchair accessible with ramps and kneeling capability. Most
subway stations are accessible via elevators or ramps, but some older stations are not fully
accessible. Riders should check station accessibility before travel, especially on the Green Line,
where several surface stops have low platforms.

## Elevators and escalators
When an elevator is out of service it can make an otherwise accessible station unusable for some
riders. The MBTA publishes elevator and escalator outages in real time as service alerts, and
provides accessible shuttle service or alternate routing when a critical elevator is down.

## The RIDE
The RIDE is the MBTA's paratransit service for people who cannot use fixed-route subway or bus
service due to a disability. It offers door-to-door, shared trips within the service area. Eligible
riders must apply and be approved. The RIDE fare is higher than a standard subway fare but is
capped by regulation.

## Reduced fares for riders with disabilities
Riders with disabilities and those holding a Transportation Access Pass (TAP) CharlieCard qualify
for reduced fares on the subway and bus, roughly half the standard fare.
